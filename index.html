Realizations

This educational tour bridged the gap between classroom theory and real-world application. Witnessing the operations at WorldTech and Rivan IT highlighted the vast potential within the cybersecurity and networking sectors. It made me realize that the industry is evolving rapidly, and staying updated is not just an option, but a necessity.

As the team at Code Chum shared, technology offers a "new perspective". Seeing how Mata Technologies uses Virtual Reality to promote tourism showed me that IT isn't just about code—it's about creating experiences. This trip was also a journey of friendship; the bonds formed with my classmates while exploring the Chocolate Hills and cruising the Loboc River are memories I will treasure forever.

Beyond the technical skills, the immersion in Bohol’s rich history and nature provided a necessary balance. We learned that while we look forward to the future of tech, we must also look back and preserve our heritage and environment.
